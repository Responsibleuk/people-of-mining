The Disaster

Samuel Bastion

In the summer of 1846, a calamitous event unfolded at East Wheal Rose, a thriving mine nestled among picturesque hills. On that fateful day, a fierce thunderstorm descended upon the area, drenching the mine and surrounding landscape with relentless rain. But what made this storm particularly bizarre was the stark contrast between the flooded mine and the dry fields just three miles away.

As the rain poured, the mine's surface quickly became submerged in water. Captain Middleton, a courageous leader, rallied a group of 300 men to create makeshift barriers around the shafts. Despite their valiant efforts, the force of the water overwhelmed their defences, causing a catastrophic flood.

In the depths of the mine, darkness engulfed the terrified miners. Candle flames were extinguished by the violent gusts, leaving them in complete blackness. Amidst the chaos, acts of heroism emerged. Captain Champion fought against the raging waters, miraculously ascending the slippery ladders to safety. Samuel Bastion, a brave timber-man, risked his life to divert the flow of water, saving eighteen lives. However, not everyone was as fortunate. One father, driven by love, ventured back into the mine to find his son, tragically losing both of them to the relentless deluge.

Above ground, rescuers worked tirelessly to bring survivors to safety. Men clung desperately to kibbles and chains, lifted to the surface like strings of onions. The mine doctor provided care and solace to the injured, amidst the overwhelming grief that hung in the air.

When the storm finally subsided, the true toll of the disaster was revealed. Thirty-nine lives had been lost, leaving behind grieving widows and fatherless children. The lower levels of the mine lay submerged, a chilling reminder of the tragedy that had unfolded.

Yet, in the face of such devastation, the indomitable spirit of East Wheal Rose prevailed. By November of that year, the debris had been cleared, and the mine was once again fully operational. It stood as a testament to the resilience and determination of the mining community, a symbol of their ability to rise above adversity and forge ahead.

The story of the East Wheal Rose disaster serves as a poignant reminder of the sacrifices made by those who toil beneath the surface, their lives forever entwined with the unpredictable forces of nature. It stands as a testament to the bravery of individuals who faced darkness and despair, and a tribute to the strength that can be found even in the darkest of days.
